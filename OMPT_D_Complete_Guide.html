What is OMPT-D?
THE EXAM IN ONE LINE
Online Mathematics Placement Test — The Most Advanced Level
OMPT-D is a globally recognised online mathematics admission test designed for students applying to highly mathematics-intensive programmes — advanced engineering, physics, pure mathematics, and theoretical computer science — at universities primarily across Europe. It is the most comprehensive and challenging test in the OMPT family.

Part 04
Registration Process — Step by Step
📋
Step 1: Check University Requirements
Confirm your target university requires OMPT-D specifically. Check their admission page or contact their admissions office. Some universities accept multiple OMPT levels; others require specifically type D for engineering programmes. Also note their passing score threshold (usually 55–65%).
🔗
Step 2: Register on omptest.org
Go to omptest.org → Register → Create your student account → Select OMPT-D → Choose your product (Test Attempt only, or Bundle including practice + mock). Each test attempt must be purchased separately. Payment is done online, securely.
📚
Step 3: Prepare Using Official Materials
Use the OMPT practice materials (if purchased). These include theory pages, exercises with automated personalised feedback, hints, and step-by-step solutions. The platform randomises values each attempt so you can practise limitlessly. Focus heavily on Differentiation (Ch 8) and Functions (Ch 6) — the largest chapters.
🧪
Step 4: Take the Mock Test
If you purchased the mock test, take it under real exam conditions — same time limit (180 min), quiet room, no distractions. You'll receive a per-topic breakdown and detailed answer sheets. This builds familiarity with the test format and helps you identify which chapters need more revision.
📅
Step 5: Schedule Your Test
After registration and preparation, schedule your test at a time and date of your choice via the OMPT platform. ProctorU will verify your identity and set up proctoring. You can reschedule up to 3 days before your chosen date if needed. Make sure you have a working webcam and stable internet.
💻
Step 6: Take the Test
On test day, log in to OMPT → click "Go to ProctorU" in your Test Info page → follow the proctor's instructions. Complete 21 questions in 180 minutes. Use scratch paper (it may be checked). Use the built-in calculator where permitted. Stay calm — 8–9 minutes per question is plenty if well prepared.
🏆
Step 7: Receive Results & Submit to University
Within 8 business days (or 4 with fast grading), you'll receive a digital certificate with your overall score and per-topic breakdown. Universities can verify your score via the OMPT admissions portal. Submit your certificate as part of your university application. If your score is below threshold, you may attempt again (up to 3 total).

Part 07
Exam Strategy & Top Tips
01
Master Differentiation First
Chapter 8 (Differentiation) has 34 topics — the most of any chapter. Product rule, quotient rule, and chain rule appear in multi-step problems across topics. If you only have limited time, prioritise this chapter above all others.
02
Don't Underestimate the Prerequisites
Chapters 1–4 are labelled "Prerequisite" but errors here cascade into wrong answers on harder questions. Ensure your algebraic manipulation, fraction work, and linear equation solving is flawless — these appear inside harder problems.
03
Use the Built-in Calculator Wisely
OMPT-D provides an online calculator. Know what it can and cannot do before the exam. Practice using the same type of calculator during preparation. Don't rely on it for algebraic steps — use it for arithmetic verification only.
04
Budget ~8.5 Minutes Per Question
With 21 questions in 180 minutes, you have roughly 8.5 minutes each. Some questions may take 5 minutes; others 12. If you spend more than 12 minutes on one question, move on and return later. Leaving a question blank costs more than a rough attempt.
05
Take the Mock Test Under Real Conditions
The mock test is your single most valuable preparation tool. Take it timed (180 min), in a quiet room, with webcam on, no interruptions. The per-topic breakdown will reveal exactly which chapters to revise — use it as a diagnostic, not just a confidence check.
06
Link Chapters: Trig + Differentiation
Chapter 9 (Trigonometry) feeds directly into Chapter 8 (Differentiation). Derivatives of sin, cos, and tan will appear as parts of larger problems. Study these together — don't treat them as isolated topics.
07
Submit Results Early
Universities like Amsterdam UC explicitly give admission priority to students who submit OMPT results before early-bird deadlines. Don't wait until the last week. Plan to take the exam at least 3–4 weeks before your application deadline.
08
Use Scratch Paper Methodically
You are allowed scratch paper (the proctor may review it). Write working clearly. For multi-step problems, label each step. Clean workings help you spot errors before final submission — and you'll thank yourself for it on longer integration/differentiation chains.
09
Know Your Inverse Functions
Inverse functions, arcsin, arccos, arctan, and inverse of exponential/log functions appear frequently across Chapters 6, 7, and 9. These are high-frequency topics that students often neglect — a few hours on these can unlock multiple marks.
